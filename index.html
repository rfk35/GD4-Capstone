Domestic violence is a pattern of coercive behavior
used by one person to gain power and control over
another in an intimate or familial relationship.

The pandemic is increasing
intimate partner violence

According to the National Coalition Against Domestic
Violence, nearly 20 people per minute are physically
abused by an intimate partner in the United States.
The pandemic intensified many of the conditions that
can fuel intimate partner violence, with many families
facing economic tension along with the stress and
uncertainty created by COVID-19.

States are Expanding
Domestic Violence Laws

The bill, which was approved by state legislators and
signed into law by Gov. Ned Lamont, broadens
Connecticut’s definition of domestic violence to
include “coercive control,” a pattern of abusive
behavior that seeks to control a victim through
emotional and psychological tactics.

Our Mission

LifeWire is here to help survivors of domestic violence find safety, stability, and recovery.We aim to change community ideas, attitudes, and actions in order to stop domestic abuse. Domestic abuse is a violation of human rights.

LifeWire envisions a society where everyone lives in a safe, oppression-free environment and has the ability to grow.

Why is it so hard to leave

Understanding the many barriers that stand in the way of a victim leaving an abusive
relationship – whether they are psychological, emotional, financial, or physical threats –
allows us to begin to support and empower victims to make the best decision for themselves
while holding abusers solely accountable for their behavior.

We support survivors of DV

Since 1982, we have partnered with more than 152,000 domestic violence survivors on their courageous journeys towards safety, stability, and healing. By offering one-on-one advocacy and support, we work each day to ensure the safety and enduring independence of every survivor who walks through our doors.
